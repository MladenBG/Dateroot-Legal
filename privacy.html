Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 18, 2026

1. Information We Collect

To help you make meaningful connections on Chat and Date Root, we collect some information about you, such as basic profile details and the types of people you’d like to meet.

  • Account Data: When you create an account, you provide us with basic login credentials (email, password), as well as basic details necessary for the service to work (gender, date of birth).
  • Profile Data: You can add additional information to your profile, such as details on your personality, lifestyle, interests, and photos.
  • Usage Data: We collect information about your activity on our services (e.g., date and time you logged in, clicks, and searches).

Payment Information & Security

WE DO NOT COLLECT, STORE, OR PROCESS YOUR CREDIT CARD DETAILS.

All financial transactions are handled entirely by our secure third-party payment processors.

When you make a purchase, your payment data is entered directly into their secure encrypted systems. Chat and Date Root only receives a "Success" or "Failure" confirmation. We never see or save your card number, CVV, or bank details.

2. How We Use Information

The main reason we use your information is to deliver and improve our services. Additionally, we use your info to help keep you safe.

  • To administer your account and provide our services to you.
  • To help you connect with other members.
  • To detect and prevent fraud or other illegal activities.
  • To facilitate payments via our secure partners (as detailed above).

3. Sharing Your Information

Since our goal is to help you make meaningful connections, the main sharing of users' information is, of course, with other members.

We also share strictly necessary technical data with service providers who assist us in operating the services (such as hosting and security). We do not sell your personal data to third-party advertisers.

4. Data Security

We work hard to protect you from unauthorized access to or alteration of your personal information. We use industry-standard encryption (SSL/TLS) for all data in transit. However, no technology is 100% secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.
